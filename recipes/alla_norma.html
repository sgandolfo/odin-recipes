Spaghetti Carbonara

Description

The most iconic Roman dish, the spaghetti Carbonara, captures the essence of the Eternal City in every forkful.

Its silky texture and bold, savory flavor have made it a timeless symbol of Italian culinary tradition.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti
  • Guanciale
  • Eggs
  • Pecorino Romano
  • Black Pepper

Steps

  1. Toast the black pepper corns in a dry pan over a medium heat for 2 minutes to release the flavours
  2. Put the toasted black peppers corns in a pestel and mortar and grind the pepper corns
  3. Cook the spaghetti in boiling, salted, water following the instructions on the package. Reserve some of the cooking water!
  4. Remove the skin from the guanciale and slice the guaciale into bite-sized chunks
  5. Fry the guanciale in a dry pan, over a medium heat, until golden and crunchy. Set aside and reserve some of the rendered fat from the guanciale
  6. Split the eggs and add the grated pecorine cheese and the ground black pepper to the egg yolks. Also add some of the reserved guanciale fat to the mixture
  7. When the spaghetti is al dente, drain the pasta and mix in the egg mixture and some of the reserved cooking water in the pan that was used to fry the guanciale. Make sure the heat is turned off!
  8. Mix well and add the guanciale to the pan, reserving some for decoration
  9. Serve with the remaining guanciale and some grated pecorine cheese
